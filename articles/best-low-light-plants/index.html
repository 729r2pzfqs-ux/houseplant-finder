10 Best Plants for Low Light Rooms

No sunny window? These houseplants thrive in dark corners and north-facing rooms.

Not every home has abundant natural light, but that doesn't mean you can't enjoy houseplants. These varieties have adapted to grow under forest canopies and actually prefer indirect or low light conditions.

Top 10 Low Light Champions

1. ZZ Plant

Nearly indestructible. Thrives on neglect and tolerates very low light. Water monthly.

2. Snake Plant

Architectural and tough. Survives in any light condition from low to bright.

3. Pothos

Fast-growing trailing vine. Adapts to low light (growth slows but it survives).

4. Cast Iron Plant

8. Parlor Palm

Elegant palm that's been popular since Victorian times for shady parlors.

9. Heartleaf Philodendron

Heart-shaped leaves on trailing vines. Tolerates low light and irregular watering.

10. Spider Plant

Classic houseplant that adapts to most conditions. Produces cute baby plants.

Tips for Low Light Success

  • Water less frequently - Plants in low light grow slower and need less water
  • Avoid fertilizing in winter - Growth naturally slows in darker months
  • Rotate your plants - Give all sides equal light exposure
  • Clean the leaves - Dust blocks light absorption
  • Consider grow lights - Supplement natural light if needed
