Best Air-Purifying Houseplants

Plants that help clean your indoor air naturally.

NASA's Clean Air Study identified several houseplants that effectively remove common indoor pollutants like formaldehyde, benzene, and trichloroethylene. Here are the top performers:

Top Air-Purifying Plants

Peace Lily

Removes formaldehyde, benzene, and ammonia. One of the most effective air cleaners.

Rubber Plant

Large leaves absorb more pollutants. Removes formaldehyde effectively.

Boston Fern

Excellent at removing formaldehyde. Also adds humidity to dry rooms.

How Many Plants Do You Need?

NASA recommends at least one plant per 100 square feet for noticeable air cleaning benefits. For a typical room, 2-3 medium plants should help improve air quality.
