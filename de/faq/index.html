Watering & Care

Wie oft sollte ich meine Zimmerpflanzen gie√üen?

It varies by plant type:

  • Succulents/Cacti: Every 2-3 weeks, let dry completely
  • Tropical plants: Weekly, when top inch is dry
  • Ferns: Keep consistently moist, never soggy
  • Snake/ZZ plants: Every 2-3 weeks, drought tolerant

Pro tip: Stick your finger 1-2 inches into the soil. If dry, water thoroughly until it drains from the bottom. If moist, wait a few more days.

Should I mist my houseplants?

Misting provides only temporary humidity. For humidity-loving plants like Calathea, Ferns, and tropical plants, better options include:

  • Use a humidifier (most effective)
  • Create a pebble tray with water under the pot
  • Group plants together to create a humid microclimate
  • Place plants in naturally humid rooms like bathrooms

Misting can help clean dusty leaves and is enjoyed by some plants, but it's not a humidity solution.

When should I repot my houseplant?

Signs it's time to repot:

  • Roots growing out of drainage holes
  • Roots circling the pot bottom (pot-bound)
  • Water runs straight through without absorbing
  • Plant growth has slowed significantly
  • Soil dries out very quickly

Best practices: Repot in spring. Go up only 1-2 inches in pot size. Use fresh, well-draining soil. Most plants need repotting every 1-2 years.

Troubleshooting

Why are my plant's leaves turning yellow?

Yellow leaves can indicate several issues:

  • Overwatering ‚Äî Most common cause. Check if soil is soggy.
  • Underwatering ‚Äî Soil completely dry? Water more often.
  • Insufficient light ‚Äî Move closer to a window.
  • Natural aging ‚Äî Lower leaves yellow naturally. Remove them.
  • Nutrient deficiency ‚Äî Try a balanced fertilizer.

Check soil moisture first ‚Äî watering issues are the most common culprit!

What causes brown tips on plant leaves?

Brown leaf tips are usually caused by:

  • Low humidity ‚Äî Increase humidity with a humidifier or pebble tray
  • Underwatering ‚Äî Water more consistently
  • Tap water minerals ‚Äî Use filtered or distilled water
  • Over-fertilizing ‚Äî Flush soil with water

Brown tips can be trimmed with clean scissors. Cut just before the brown, following the leaf's natural shape.

How do I know if my plant needs more light?

Signs your plant needs more light:

  • Leggy growth ‚Äî Long, stretched stems reaching toward light
  • Smaller leaves ‚Äî New leaves are smaller than old ones
  • Slow/no growth ‚Äî Plant seems dormant even in growing season
  • Loss of variegation ‚Äî Variegated plants turn solid green
  • Leaning ‚Äî Plant leans toward the light source

Move it closer to a window or consider a grow light for very dark spaces.

Why is my plant dropping leaves?

Leaf drop can be caused by:

  • Acclimation stress ‚Äî New plants often drop leaves while adjusting. Give it time!
  • Overwatering ‚Äî Check soil moisture and drainage
  • Temperature/draft shock ‚Äî Avoid cold drafts and heating vents
  • Moving the plant ‚Äî Some plants hate being moved (looking at you, Fiddle Leaf Fig)
  • Pest infestation ‚Äî Check undersides of leaves for bugs

Advanced Care

How do I propagate houseplants?

Common propagation methods:

  • Water propagation: Cut stem below a node, place in water until roots appear (Pothos, Philodendron, Monstera)
  • Leaf cuttings: Lay a leaf on soil, wait for babies (Succulents)
  • Division: Separate root clumps into individual plants (Snake Plant, ZZ Plant)
  • Plantlets: Pot up baby plants that form naturally (Spider Plant)

Spring and summer are the best times to propagate when plants are actively growing.
